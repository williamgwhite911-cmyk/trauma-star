Stroke (CVA)

Ischemic · hemorrhagic · undetermined
TS-NEU-001 · §6 Neurology
Recognize & assess (all strokes)
  • BE-FAST — Balance, Eyes, Face, Arm, Speech, Time; plus an LVO screen (RACE / LAMS / VAN) per your system.
  • Last Known Well (LKW) — exact time drives tPA/thrombectomy eligibility. Get a witness/phone number.
  • Glucose — always check; hypoglycemia mimics stroke (treat if < 60 mg/dL).
  • Anticoagulant / antiplatelet use, meds, PMH; cardiac monitor (AF).
  • ⚠ Field cannot distinguish ischemic vs hemorrhagic without CT — see pathways below.
Common actions
  • O₂ only to keep SpO₂ ≥ 94% (avoid hyperoxia); protect airway; suction PRN.
  • Position: HOB ~30° if ↓LOC, vomiting, or suspected ICP; otherwise supine as tolerated.
  • IV access; avoid hypotension & hypoxia; treat hypoglycemia; avoid hyperthermia.
  • Pre-notify the receiving stroke center; pick destination by suspected LVO + time window.
Pathways — BP & destination
Ischemic (suspected)
Blood pressure — permissive hypertension
Do not routinely lower. Treat only if SBP > 220 or DBP > 120 (cautious lowering per med control). Thrombolysis candidate: target < 185/110.
Destination / time
tPA window ≤ 4.5 h from LKW; thrombectomy for LVO up to 24 h (selected) → thrombectomy-capable / comprehensive stroke center.
Hemorrhagic (suspected ICH)
Clues
Severe headache, vomiting, rapid ↓LOC, very high BP, anticoagulated, posturing/herniation signs.
Blood pressure — controlled lowering
If SBP 150–220: target ~130–150, avoid < 130. Higher: reduce ~20% in 1st hr, smooth control — per med control.
Other
HOB 30°, avoid hypoxia/hypotension, anticipate ICP/herniation; anticoagulation reversal at receiving → neurosurgical-capable center.
Undetermined (can't differentiate in field)
Default — supportive, don't over-treat BP
Do not aggressively lower BP unless extreme (SBP > 220) or clear herniation; over-lowering harms the ischemic penumbra. Contact medical control.
Priority
Rapid transport to a CT/stroke-capable center, pre-notify, document LKW & exam — let CT define the type.
Cautions & red flags
  • Check glucose first — hypoglycemia mimics stroke.
  • Don't delay transport — time is brain; LKW drives every decision.
  • Avoid hypotension and hypoxia — both worsen ischemic and hemorrhagic stroke.
  • Anticoagulant use raises ICH risk — relay it to receiving.
  • Herniation (blown pupil, posturing, Cushing reflex) → ICP measures, elevate HOB, notify early.
  • Posterior/basilar stroke (balance, eyes, vertigo) can be subtle — use BE-FAST.
Documentation — medically necessary
  • Last Known Well time + how determined (witness/contact).
  • Stroke scale type & score; LVO screen result.
  • Glucose; serial BP, GCS/neuro exam, pupils.
  • Anticoagulant/antiplatelet & med list, PMH.
  • Destination rationale (LVO / time window) and pre-notification time.
  • Interventions (O₂, glucose, any BP med + order source) and response.
  • Why rotor-wing / this level of care (thrombectomy window, distance to capable center).
Overview

Stroke is time-critical — "time is brain." About 87% are ischemic (vessel occlusion) and the remainder hemorrhagic (intracerebral or subarachnoid). The two require opposite blood-pressure strategies, yet they cannot be reliably distinguished without CT. The prehospital goals are rapid recognition, supportive care, accurate Last-Known-Well, and delivery to the right imaging/intervention-capable center as fast as possible.1

Pathophysiology / rationale

In ischemic stroke a perfusion-dependent penumbra surrounds the core; elevated BP helps maintain collateral flow, so permissive hypertension is favored until reperfusion.1 In intracerebral hemorrhage, higher BP promotes hematoma expansion, so smooth, early BP control improves outcomes — while excessive lowering (< 130 mmHg) can cause harm.2 Because the field strategies diverge and the type is unknown without imaging, the "undetermined" pathway defaults to supportive care and rapid transport rather than aggressive BP manipulation.

In-flight considerations
  • Continuous BP and neuro monitoring; protect airway in ↓LOC; HOB 30° when ICP is a concern.
  • Choose destination by suspected LVO and the thrombolysis/thrombectomy time window; pre-notify so the team is ready on arrival.1,3
  • From the Keys, the ground-vs-air time differential is decisive for reaching a comprehensive stroke / thrombectomy center within window.
  • Avoid hypoxia, hypotension, hyperthermia, and glucose extremes throughout.
Management — detail

Use a validated stroke scale plus an LVO screen and establish Last-Known-Well; provide advance notification to the receiving hospital, which shortens treatment times and improves outcomes.1,3 For suspected ischemic stroke do not routinely lower BP; antihypertensives are generally reserved for SBP > 220 or DBP > 120, or to reach < 185/110 when thrombolysis is planned.1 For suspected ICH with SBP 150–220, target roughly 130–150 mmHg and avoid dropping below 130; for higher pressures, lower by about 20% in the first hour with smooth control.2 When the type is undetermined — the usual prehospital situation — manage supportively, treat glucose, avoid hypoxia/hypotension, and expedite imaging rather than aggressively manipulating BP; obtain online medical control for any BP intervention.1,2
