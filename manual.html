§1 — Airway & Ventilation

TS-AW-001
Ketamine-Forward Trauma Airway (RSI)
Indications: RSI for trauma / critical-care transport; choose pathway by hemodynamics (normotensive: MAP ≥ 65 & SBP ≥ 100; hypotensive: MAP < 65 or SBP < 100).
Normotensive: Ketamine 1–2 mg/kg IV; Rocuronium 1–1.2 mg/kg IV. Post-intubation ketamine drip start 1 mg/kg/hr (titrate 1–4); midazolam add-on 1–2 mg then 1–10 mg/hr.
Hypotensive: push-dose epi bridge (see TS-HD-001) / norepinephrine; Ketamine 0.5–1 mg/kg IV; Rocuronium 1–1.2 mg/kg; resuscitate to MAP ≥ 65 / SBP ≥ 100; same post-intubation sedation.
Key points: confirm post-intubation sedation before paralysis; rocuronium outlasts ketamine — never leave a paralyzed patient unsedated.
Source: THOR / peri-intubation & post-intubation sedation literature.

§2 — Hemodynamics & Shock

TS-HD-001
Push-Dose Pressors
Indications: transient hypotension / peri-intubation bridge to a continuous infusion.
Epinephrine: mix 1 mg in 100 mL NS/D5W = 10 mcg/mL; give 0.5–2 mL (5–20 mcg) IV q2–5 min.
Phenylephrine: 100 mcg/mL; give 0.5–2 mL (50–200 mcg) IV q2–5 min.
Key points: label syringe + concentration; bridge only — transition to infusion early.
Source: push-dose pressor consensus / EM literature.
TS-HD-002
Whole Blood — Field Administration
Indications: hemorrhagic shock — SBP < 90, or HR ≥ 110 with SBP ≤ 90, or class III–IV shock from hemorrhage.
Product: LTOWB 1 unit (~500 mL) IV/IO, titrate to radial pulse / SBP ~90 (TBI: SBP ≥ 110). Calcium CaCl₂ 1 g or Ca gluconate 3 g with 1st unit; TXA 1 g if < 3 h.
Key points: warm to ~38 °C (≤ 42); NaCl-only line (no LR/Ca); two-person unit verification; treat hypothermia/hypocalcemia.
Source: THOR-AABB prehospital recommendations; JTS Damage Control Resuscitation; EAST whole-blood guideline.

§3 — Pediatrics

TS-PED-001
Pediatric Resuscitation (Broselow / Handtevy)
Indications: pediatric resuscitation; weight from Broselow tape / Handtevy (age) / scale.
Core doses: Epi (arrest) 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO; fluids 20 mL/kg (10 in trauma); defib 2→4 J/kg; D10 5 mL/kg; naloxone 0.1 mg/kg; amiodarone 5 mg/kg; midazolam 0.1 mg/kg; ketamine 1–2 mg/kg; rocuronium 1 mg/kg.
Key points: interactive weight-based calculator; ketamine concentration default 500 mg/5 mL; color zones with weight + age ranges.
Source: standard weight-based (PALS) dosing; Broselow/Handtevy color zones (visual aid).

§4 — Operations & Transport

TS-OPS-001
IFT Transport Times — Ground vs AW139
Reference: Keys → Miami corridor (single highway, US-1). Ground > 4 h (~160 mi from Lower Keys) vs AW139 ~45–50 min — ~3+ hours saved.
Use: supports the air-vs-ground medical-necessity rationale; confirm actual mileage/ETA per transport (GPS / Google Maps).
Source: operational planning reference (estimates).

§5 — Environmental & Diving

TS-ENV-001
Decompression Sickness (Bends)
Indications: dive injury with joint/limb pain, skin, neuro, or cardiopulmonary symptoms after a dive.
Treatment: assume DCI; notify pilot for lowest feasible cabin altitude; 100% O₂ 15 L/min continuous; supine; IV fluids LR or NS 150 mL/hr; DAN +1-919-684-9111 + nearest chamber.
Flight (rotor-wing): fly < 1000 ft / near sea-level; continuous O₂. No nitrous oxide.
Source: DAN; UHMS best-practice; 2018 pre-hospital DCI consensus; aeromedical transport literature.

§6 — Neurology

TS-NEU-001
Stroke — Ischemic
Assess: BE-FAST + LVO screen; Last Known Well; glucose; anticoagulant use.
BP: no tPA/intervention — treat only SBP > 220 or DBP > 120 (limit reduction 15% baseline). To qualify for / post-tPA: ≤ 180/105. Post-thrombectomy: per interventionalist.
Alteplase (online order): 0.9 mg/kg TBW (max 90 mg) — 10% over 1 min, 90% over 60 min; < 4.5 h, age ≥ 18.
Antihypertensives: Nicardipine 5 mg/hr, ↑2.5 q5–15, max 15; Clevidipine 1–2 mg/hr, max 32; Labetalol 20 mg, repeat 40/80, max 300.
Source: PHI §7.2 Acute Ischemic Stroke; AHA/ASA AIS guideline.
TS-NEU-002
Stroke — Hemorrhagic (ICH)
BP: ICH with SBP > 160 → lower to 130–160 mmHg, smooth control.
Antihypertensives: Nicardipine / Clevidipine / Labetalol (as above); Esmolol 500 mcg/kg load, 50 mcg/kg/min titration, max 300 mcg/kg/min.
Key points: anticoagulant reversal under direct medical control; HOB 30°; neurosurgical-capable destination.
Source: PHI §7.3 Hemorrhagic Stroke; AHA/ASA ICH guideline.
TS-NEU-003
Stroke — Undetermined
BP: positive stroke assessment + glucose ≥ 60 → SBP goal < 185; antihypertensives as above (Nicardipine / Clevidipine / Labetalol).
Key points: hypoglycemia mimics stroke — check/treat first; rapid transport to CT to define type.
Source: PHI §7.4 Undifferentiated Stroke.
TS-NEU-004
Seizures / Status Epilepticus
Glucose first. Benzodiazepine q2–3 min until controlled (IV preferred): Lorazepam 4 mg IV/IM/NAS (peds 0.1 mg/kg, max 4); Midazolam 0.1 mg/kg IV (max 5) or 0.2 mg/kg IM/NAS (max 10).
Refractory (choose one): Levetiracetam 4 g IV over 15 min (peds 60 mg/kg); Fosphenytoin 15 mg PE/kg; Phenytoin 15 mg/kg. Infusions (propofol/phenobarb/benzo) under direct medical control.
Key points: paralytics do NOT treat seizures.
Source: PHI §7.1 Seizures & §7.1.1 Refractory Seizures.
TS-NEU-005
Brain Herniation Syndrome
Recognize: abnormal breathing, pupillary changes, posturing, Cushing reflex (bradycardia + HTN + resp depression).
Treatment: Sodium Chloride 3% 250 mL IV over 5–15 min (peds 3–5 mL/kg IBW). Alternate Mannitol 0.5–1 g/kg over 30–60 min (online order). HOB 30°, optimize ventilation/oxygenation.
Source: PHI §7.5 Brain Herniation Syndrome.
TS-NEU-006
CNS Infection
Treatment (ASAP): Cefotaxime 50 mg/kg IV over 3–5 min PLUS — Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg (max 2 g) if ≥ 3 months, or Ampicillin 100 mg/kg if < 3 months.
Key points: do not delay antibiotics; treat sepsis/seizures; droplet precautions.
Source: PHI §7.6 CNS Infection.

§7 — Toxicology

TS-TOX-001
Opioid Overdose
Treatment: ventilate first; Naloxone — adult 1–2 mg IV/IM/IN, peds 0.01 mg/kg (max 1 mg), newborn 0.1 mg/kg. Infusion: ⅔ of the effective dose each hour.
Key points: titrate to respiration, not full arousal; watch for re-sedation.
Source: PHI §9.1.1 Opioid Overdose.
TS-TOX-002
Smoke Inhalation & Cyanide
Suspect: enclosed-space fire + AMS, seizures, SBP < 90/shock, arrest, lactate ≥ 8, or pH < 7.25 — treat empirically.
Cyanokit (hydroxocobalamin): adult 5 g in 100 mL NS over 15 min (peds 70 mg/kg, max 5 g); repeat ×1, max 10 g.
Concurrent: 100% O₂ (CO); hyperbaric for severe CO; early airway for burns/edema; treat bronchospasm, seizures, hypotension.
Source: PHI §9.1.2; ACEP / LITFL / EM literature.
TS-TOX-003
TCA Overdose
Treat if: CV instability/hypotension, seizures/coma, wide QRS, prolonged QT, ventricular dysrhythmia.
Sodium Bicarbonate: adult 50–100 mEq IV over 5–10 min (peds 1–2 mEq/kg); 1 dose standing, repeats need physician order.
Source: PHI §9.1.3 TCA Overdose.
TS-TOX-004
Envenomation (snake / scorpion)
General: mark swelling border + time; immobilize below heart; measure circumference q15 min.
Pit viper: CroFab 4–6 vials in 250 mL NS; start 25–50 mL/hr ×10 min, then 250 mL/hr.
Scorpion: Atropine 1 mg (peds 0.02 mg/kg, max 0.5) for secretions; Fentanyl 0.5–2 mcg/kg or Midazolam for clonus/sedation; avoid long-acting opioids/benzos & anticholinergics.
Source: PHI §9.2 Envenomation.
