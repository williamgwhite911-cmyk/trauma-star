Ketamine-Forward Trauma Airway

Adult RSI · post-intubation transport
TS-AW-001 · §1 Airway & Ventilation
Computed doses (green) appear beside each weight-based med. Verify before administering.
Normotensive
MAP ≥ 65
AND SBP ≥ 100
Hypotensive
MAP < 65
OR SBP < 100
Before drugs
  • Monitor: ECG, SpO₂, BP, ETCO₂ when available.
  • Prepare suction, VL/DL, bougie, backup airway, and a cric plan.
  • Preoxygenate with best tolerated method.
  • Confirm the post-intubation sedation plan before paralysis.
  • In hemorrhagic shock: blood and hemorrhage control first.
Normotensive pathway · MAP ≥ 65 and SBP ≥ 100
Ketamine IV — induction
1–2 mg/kg
Rocuronium IV — paralytic
1–1.2 mg/kg
Ketamine drip IV — post-intubation sedation
Start 1 mg/kg/hr, titrate 1–4 mg/kg/hr.
Midazolam IV — add-on amnesia/sedation
1–2 mg bolus, then 1–10 mg/hr or 0.02–0.1 mg/kg/hr.
Breakthrough agitation
Midazolam 2 mg IV q15 min PRN, max 4 doses in 60 min.
Hypotensive pathway · MAP < 65 or SBP < 100
Pressor bridge
Push-dose epinephrine
Mix 1 mg in 100 mL NS/D5W = 10 mcg/mL. Give 1 mL = 10 mcg IV push, repeat q2–3 min PRN.
Epinephrine drip
Start 10 mL/hr ≈ 1.7 mcg/min, titrate to effect.
Norepinephrine
Preferred continuous vasopressor if time and staffing allow.
Induction
Ketamine IV — reduced dose
0.5–1 mg/kg
Rocuronium IV — paralytic
1–1.2 mg/kg
Post-intubation
Resuscitation target
Continue pressors/resuscitation to at least MAP 65 and SBP 100 when feasible.
Ketamine drip IV
Start 1 mg/kg/hr, titrate 1–4 mg/kg/hr.
Midazolam IV — add-on (caution in shock)
1–2 mg bolus, then 1–10 mg/hr or 0.02–0.1 mg/kg/hr.
Breakthrough agitation
Midazolam 2 mg IV q15 min PRN, max 4 doses in 60 min — extra caution in shock.
Safety reminders
  • Rocuronium outlasts ketamine — never leave a paralyzed patient without sedation.
  • Use clearly labeled pressor concentrations only.
  • Titrate all post-intubation meds to response, RASS, and hemodynamics.
