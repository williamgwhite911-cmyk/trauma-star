Brain Herniation Syndrome

↑ ICP · impending herniation
TS-NEU-005 · Sec 6 Neurology
Recognize — signs of herniation
  • Breathing patterns — Cheyne-Stokes, central hyperventilation, ataxic.
  • Pupils — asymmetric dilation, fixed.
  • Motor — decerebrate (extensor) or decorticate (flexor) posturing, hemiparesis/plegia, flaccidity.
  • Cushing reflex — bradycardia + respiratory depression + hypertension.
Treatment — hyperosmolar therapy
Sodium Chloride 3% — first line
Adult 250 mL IV over 5–15 min · Peds 3–5 mL/kg IBW IV over 5–15 min
Mannitol — alternate · online order
Adult 0.5–1 g/kg IV over 30–60 min · Peds: refer to adult dosing. Requires online order.
Supportive / ICP measures
  • HOB 30°, head midline; avoid venous obstruction (tight collar/ties).
  • Optimize oxygenation/ventilation — avoid hypoxia and hypotension (secondary injury).
  • Treat per medical control; brief controlled hyperventilation only as a temporizing measure for active herniation per protocol.
  • Manage collaboratively with treating provider / PHI–style medical direction where practicable.
Documentation — medically necessary
  • Herniation signs (breathing, pupils, motor, Cushing) with times; serial GCS/pupils.
  • Hyperosmolar agent — drug, dose, route, time; online order for mannitol.
  • HOB/positioning, ventilation parameters (EtCO₂), oxygenation.
  • Suspected cause (ICH, stroke, mass, hydrocephalus); destination (neurosurgical).
  • Why rotor-wing / level of care.
Overview

Brain herniation may occur with expanding mass lesions — intracranial hemorrhage, stroke, abscess, tumor, or hydrocephalus. Recognizing the syndrome early and reducing intracranial pressure with hyperosmolar therapy buys time to reach definitive neurosurgical care. Manage collaboratively with the treating provider / medical direction where practicable.

Management — detail

When herniation signs are present (abnormal breathing, abnormal pupils, posturing, or the Cushing reflex), give hyperosmolar therapy: 3% sodium chloride is first line — 250 mL IV over 5–15 min in adults, or 3–5 mL/kg ideal body weight over 5–15 min in children. Mannitol 0.5–1 g/kg IV over 30–60 min is an alternate that requires an online order. Pair with standard ICP measures — head-up positioning, midline head, optimized oxygenation/ventilation, and avoidance of hypotension/hypoxia — and expedite transport to a neurosurgical-capable center.
