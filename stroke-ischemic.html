Stroke — Ischemic

Suspected acute ischemic stroke
TS-NEU-001 · §6 Neurology
Ischemic Hemorrhagic Undetermined
Recognize & assess
  • BE-FAST + LVO screen (RACE / LAMS / VAN); focal deficit, often sudden.
  • Last Known Well (LKW) — drives tPA / thrombectomy eligibility; get a witness/contact.
  • Glucose — always check (hypoglycemia mimic; treat if < 60).
  • Anticoagulant / antiplatelet use; cardiac monitor (AF is a common source).
Blood pressure management
No tPA / no intervention
Treat only if SBP > 220 or DBP > 120. Limit BP reduction to 15% of baseline in the first 24 h (standing order).
To qualify for tPA
Reduce & maintain ≤ 180/105 before thrombolytics (standing order).
Post-tPA
Maintain ≤ 180/105 for ≥ 24 h (standing order).
Post-thrombectomy
Per the interventionalist (online order). Contact medical control for ICH signs or sudden neuro deterioration after alteplase.
Destination & time window
tPA / thrombolysis
≤ 4.5 h from LKW.
Thrombectomy (LVO)
up to 24 h (selected) → thrombectomy-capable / comprehensive stroke center. Pre-notify.
Thrombolytic — Alteplase (online order)
Alteplase — acute ischemic stroke (adult)
0.9 mg/kg TBW IV (max 90 mg) — give 10% over 1 min, then 90% over 60 min via pump. Dose by total body weight. Requires online physician order.
Inclusion
Ischemic stroke with deficit · within 4.5 h of onset · age ≥ 18. (No pediatric standing orders.)
Key exclusions
Hemorrhage on CT · prior ICH · stroke/severe head trauma < 3 mo · intracranial surgery/neoplasm · GI bleed < 21 d · SBP ≥ 185 or DBP ≥ 105 · platelets < 100k · INR > 1.7 / PT > 15 s / aPTT > 40 s · suspected SAH or aortic dissection.
Antihypertensives — choose one (PHI doses)
Nicardipine
5 mg/hr IV, ↑ 2.5 mg/hr q5–15 min PRN, titrate to target, max 15 mg/hr (may ↓ to 3 mg/hr after target).
Clevidipine
1–2 mg/hr IV; may double q90 s, then slow as BP nears goal; max 32 mg/hr.
Labetalol
20 mg IV over 2 min; repeat 40 mg in 10–20 min, then 80 mg q10–20 min (×3) PRN; or 2 mg/min infusion; max total 300 mg.
Supportive care
  • O₂ only to keep SpO₂ ≥ 94%; protect airway; suction PRN.
  • HOB ~30° if ↓LOC/vomiting; otherwise supine as tolerated.
  • IV access; avoid hypotension & hypoxia; treat hypoglycemia; avoid hyperthermia.
Cautions & red flags
  • Avoid hypotension/hypoxia — they extend the infarct.
  • Check glucose first — hypoglycemia mimics stroke.
  • Time is brain — don't delay transport; LKW governs eligibility.
  • Reassess for conversion to hemorrhage (sudden ↓LOC, severe HA, vomiting) → switch pathway.
Documentation — medically necessary
  • Last Known Well time + how determined.
  • Stroke scale + score; LVO screen result.
  • Glucose; serial BP, GCS/neuro exam, pupils.
  • Anticoagulant/antiplatelet & med list.
  • Destination rationale (LVO / window) + pre-notification time.
  • Any BP intervention + order source and response.
  • Why rotor-wing / level of care (thrombectomy window, distance).
Overview

Ischemic stroke (~87% of strokes) results from vessel occlusion; an early, perfusion-dependent penumbra surrounds the infarct core. Rapid recognition, accurate Last-Known-Well, and delivery to a thrombolysis/thrombectomy-capable center drive outcome.1

Pathophysiology / rationale

The penumbra is salvageable if reperfused promptly; collateral flow is pressure-dependent, so permissive hypertension is favored and routine BP lowering is avoided. Per PHI guidance, antihypertensives are reserved for SBP > 220 / DBP > 120 (limit reduction to 15% of baseline), or to reach ≤ 180/105 when thrombolysis is planned and for 24 h after.1,3

In-flight considerations
  • Select destination by suspected LVO and the thrombolysis/thrombectomy window; pre-notify so the team is ready.1,2
  • From the Keys, the air-vs-ground time differential is often decisive for staying in window.
  • Maintain SpO₂ ≥ 94%, avoid hypotension; continuous neuro/BP monitoring.
Management — detail

Use a validated stroke scale + LVO screen, fix LKW, and provide advance notification (shortens treatment times, improves outcomes).1,2 Do not routinely lower BP; reserve treatment for the thresholds above. Treat hypoglycemia, maintain oxygenation/perfusion, and expedite transport to the appropriate center. Obtain online medical control for any BP intervention.1
