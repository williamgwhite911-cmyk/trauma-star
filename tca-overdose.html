Cautions & red flags
  • Treat TCA arrhythmias with bicarbonate first (narrows QRS, overcomes Na-channel blockade).
  • Watch for rapid deterioration — seizures, hypotension, dysrhythmia; anticipate airway needs.
  • Avoid Class IA/IC and other QRS-prolonging antiarrhythmics.
Documentation — medically necessary
  • Agent/amount/time; ECG (QRS width, QT), rhythm, BP, mental status.
  • Sodium bicarbonate — dose, route, time, response (QRS, BP); physician order for repeats.
  • Seizure/airway management; Poison Control contact.
  • Why rotor-wing / level of care.
Overview

TCA overdose causes sodium-channel blockade (wide QRS, ventricular dysrhythmia), anticholinergic effects, seizures, and refractory hypotension. Sodium bicarbonate is the key intervention for cardiotoxicity.

Management — detail

When cardiovascular instability, hypotension, rapid CNS deterioration (seizures/coma), prolonged QT, widened QRS, or ventricular dysrhythmias occur, treat TCA-related arrhythmias first with sodium bicarbonate — adult 50–100 mEq IV over 5–10 min (max single 100 mEq), pediatric 1–2 mEq/kg IV over 5–10 min. One dose may be given on standing order; additional doses require a physician order. Support airway and seizures, and avoid agents that further prolong the QRS.
