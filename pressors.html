Push-Dose Pressors

Transient hypotension · peri-intubation bridge
TS-HD-001 · Sec 2 Hemodynamics & Shock
When to use
  • Short-lived hypotension while a continuous vasopressor is being prepared.
  • Peri-intubation / post-intubation hypotension.
  • Bridge only — start a continuous infusion (norepinephrine/epinephrine) for sustained shock.
  • Reassess blood pressure every 2–3 minutes; treat the cause (volume, blood, tension PTX).
Push-dose epinephrine
Epinephrine
10 mcg/mL · mixed (α + β)
Mix
1 mg epinephrine in 100 cc NS or D5W = 10 mcg/mL.
Dose
0.5–2 mL = 5–20 mcg IV every 2–5 min, titrate to MAP/perfusion.
Onset / duration
Onset ~1 min · duration ~5–10 min. Use when some chronotropy/inotropy is desired.
Push-dose phenylephrine
Phenylephrine
100 mcg/mL · pure α
Mix
Draw 1 mL of 10 mg/mL into 100 mL NS → 100 mcg/mL.
Dose
0.5–2 mL = 50–200 mcg IV every 2–5 min, titrate to effect.
Onset / duration
Onset ~1 min · duration ~10–20 min. Pure vasoconstrictor — useful when tachycardic; may cause reflex bradycardia.
Safety
  • Label the syringe with drug + concentration. Extravasation and dosing errors are the main risks.
  • Push-dose is a temporizing bridge — transition to a continuous infusion early.
  • Confirm concentration before every push; never give the undiluted ampule.
Reference only. Follow Trauma Star medical direction and current MCFR protocol. Verify drug, concentration, and dose before administration.
