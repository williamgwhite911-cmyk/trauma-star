Stroke — Undetermined

Type unknown — no field CT
TS-NEU-003 · §6 Neurology
Ischemic Hemorrhagic Undetermined
The usual field situation
  • Clear stroke signs (BE-FAST +) but type cannot be determined without CT.
  • Get Last Known Well, glucose, anticoagulant/med list, LVO screen.
  • Watch for ICH clues (severe HA, vomiting, rapid ↓LOC, very high BP, herniation) — if present, manage on the Hemorrhagic pathway.
Blood pressure — default: do NOT over-treat
Don't aggressively lower BP
Over-lowering harms the ischemic penumbra and the type is unknown. Hold antihypertensives unless SBP > 220 or clear herniation — and then only per medical control.
Let CT define the type
Rapid transport to a CT / stroke-capable center is the priority — imaging decides the BP strategy.
Supportive care (safe for both types)
  • O₂ to keep SpO₂ ≥ 94%; protect airway; suction PRN.
  • HOB ~30° if ↓LOC/vomiting/ICP concern; otherwise supine as tolerated.
  • IV access; treat hypoglycemia; avoid hypotension, hypoxia, hyperthermia.
  • Pre-notify and pick destination by suspected LVO + time window.
Cautions & red flags
  • When in doubt, don't lower BP — get the order; expedite imaging.
  • Check glucose first — hypoglycemia mimics stroke.
  • Time is brain — LKW governs eligibility; don't delay transport.
  • Reassess continually; new ICH clues → Hemorrhagic pathway.
Documentation — medically necessary
  • Last Known Well + how determined; stroke scale + score; LVO screen.
  • Glucose; serial BP, GCS/neuro exam, pupils.
  • Anticoagulant/antiplatelet + med list, PMH.
  • Medical-control contact for any BP decision; destination rationale + pre-notification.
  • Interventions (O₂, glucose) and response.
  • Why rotor-wing / level of care (time-critical, distance to CT-capable center).
Overview

Without CT, the field cannot reliably separate ischemic from hemorrhagic stroke — which is the default prehospital reality. Because the two require opposite blood-pressure strategies, the safest approach when the type is unknown is supportive care plus rapid transport to imaging, rather than aggressive BP manipulation.1,2

Pathophysiology / rationale

In ischemic stroke, elevated BP supports the penumbra (permissive hypertension); in ICH, high BP drives hematoma expansion (controlled lowering). Applying the wrong strategy can worsen outcome — so when the type is undetermined, default toward not lowering BP (to avoid harming a possible ischemic penumbra) unless pressures are extreme or herniation is evident, and let CT decide.1,2

In-flight considerations
  • Prioritize speed to a CT/stroke-capable center; pre-notify so imaging is ready.1
  • Continuous neuro/BP monitoring; escalate to medical control before any BP agent.
  • Maintain oxygenation and perfusion; treat glucose; avoid hyperthermia.
Management — detail

Manage supportively: maintain SpO₂ ≥ 94%, protect the airway, treat hypoglycemia, and avoid hypotension and hyperthermia. Do not routinely lower BP when the stroke type is unknown; reserve any intervention for extreme hypertension (e.g., SBP > 220) or clear herniation, and only with online medical control. Expedite transport to a CT-capable stroke center with advance notification, then follow the imaging-confirmed pathway.1,2
